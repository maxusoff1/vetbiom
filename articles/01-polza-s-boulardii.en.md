## Saccharomyces boulardii in Animals: What Are the Benefits

**Saccharomyces boulardii** is a probiotic yeast used in animals to support gastrointestinal health. In veterinary and feed research, this microorganism is primarily considered for diarrhea support, post-antibiotic microbiota disturbances, and stress-related conditions associated with housing and transport of animals.[[pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9414249/)]

## How It Works

S. boulardii has several important mechanisms of action. It helps maintain intestinal barrier integrity, reduces inflammatory responses, influences pathogen interaction with the intestinal mucosa, and may reduce the risk of microbiota imbalance after antibiotic therapy.[[pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7344949/)]

Another practical advantage is that yeast is not a bacterium, so antibiotics generally do not suppress it directly. This makes S. boulardii a convenient option for supporting animals during or after antibacterial treatment.[[dogsnaturallymagazine](https://www.dogsnaturallymagazine.com/saccharomyces-boulardii/)]

## Where the Effect Is Most Noticeable

The most compelling data relate to the following situations:

- Diarrhea of various origins, especially acute and stress-associated diarrhea.[[saccharomycesboulardii](https://www.saccharomycesboulardii.com/chto-takoe-saharomicety-bulardi-cncm-i-745/nauchnye-dannye/)]
    
- Post-antibiotic diarrhea, when normal intestinal microflora is disrupted.[[saccharomycesboulardii](https://www.saccharomycesboulardii.com/chto-takoe-saharomicety-bulardi-cncm-i-745/nauchnye-dannye/)]
    
- Stress periods in farm animals, including transport and technological stressors.[[pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9414249/)]
    
- Supporting intestinal health in poultry and farm animals as part of feed additives.[[pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12606370/)]
    

A 2024 review explicitly notes that S. boulardii is widely considered as a feed additive for poultry and farm animals to improve intestinal condition.[[pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12606370/)]

## What Research Shows

In a review of animals under stressful conditions, S. boulardii supplementation improved intestinal status and reduced stress load. This is important because in animals, stress often triggers a chain reaction: decreased appetite, altered microflora, impaired intestinal barrier function, and diarrhea.[[pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9414249/)]

For veterinary practice, the value of S. boulardii lies in the fact that it works not as a "universal cure" but as a **supportive biological tool**: it helps the gut recover, reduces the severity of disorders, and may decrease the frequency of recurrent diarrhea episodes when used correctly.[[saccharomycesboulardii](https://www.saccharomycesboulardii.com/chto-takoe-saharomicety-bulardi-cncm-i-745/nauchnye-dannye/)]

## Which Animals Benefit

S. boulardii is most commonly discussed for:

- dogs;
    
- cats;
    
- calves;
    
- piglets;
    
- poultry;
    
- other farm animals as part of feed programs.[[dogsnaturallymagazine](https://www.dogsnaturallymagazine.com/saccharomyces-boulardii/)]
    

In clinical and applied veterinary medicine, it is especially often used for gastrointestinal issues in dogs, where probiotic yeast is considered a supportive option for antibiotic-associated and non-specific diarrhea.[[dogsnaturallymagazine](https://www.dogsnaturallymagazine.com/saccharomyces-boulardii/)]

## Limitations and Cautions

It is important to understand that S. boulardii is not a substitute for diagnostics. If an animal has blood in the stool, marked lethargy, vomiting, dehydration, fever, or rapid deterioration, a veterinary assessment of the cause is needed — not just a probiotic.[[pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9414249/)]

The effect also depends on the strain, dosage, product form, and concomitant therapy. In scientific sources, the specific strain CNCM I-745 is the most well-studied, and results for this strain cannot be automatically extrapolated to any product labeled "S. boulardii".[[saccharomycesboulardii](https://www.saccharomycesboulardii.com/chto-takoe-saharomicety-bulardi-cncm-i-745/nauchnye-dannye/)]

## Summary

**S. boulardii is beneficial for animals primarily as a gut-supporting agent**: it helps with diarrhea — especially post-antibiotic and stress-related — and promotes restoration of the intestinal barrier and microbial balance. In practice, it is one of the most promising probiotic yeast ingredients for veterinary medicine and feed additives.[[pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7344949/)]
