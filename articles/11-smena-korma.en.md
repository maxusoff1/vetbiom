# S. boulardii for Diet Change: How to Prevent Diarrhea in Cats and Dogs

With an abrupt **diet change diarrhea** is a frequent consequence of intestinal microbiota imbalance. Scientific evidence shows that the probiotic **S. boulardii for cats during diet change** and **S. boulardii for dogs during diet change** reduces the risk and duration of diarrhea by supporting the gut during the adaptation period.[[pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11176121/)]

## Why Diet Change Causes Diarrhea

An abrupt **diet change** disrupts the composition of the intestinal microbiome and metabolic profile. In dogs transitioning to a new diet, metabolites change within 2 days, while complete microbiome stabilization takes 6–10 days. In cats, a similar imbalance triggers temporary enzyme insufficiency, increased intestinal barrier permeability, and overgrowth of opportunistic flora, clinically manifesting as **diet change diarrhea**.[[zooinform](https://zooinform.ru/vete/issledovanie-pokazalo-chto-dobavki-s-probiotikami-i-kletchatkoj-uluchshayut-pishhevarenie-u-koshek/)]

The mechanism involves new substrates (proteins, fats, carbohydrates) requiring different enzymatic activity and bacterial species. Without adaptation, osmotic and secretory diarrhea develops.[[applied-research](https://applied-research.ru/article/view?id=11927)]

## How to Support the Gut: Probiotic During Diet Change

**Probiotic during diet change** based on _Saccharomyces boulardii_ CNCM I-745 is officially recommended for the prevention and treatment of diarrhea in humans and animals with evidence level A. Key effects:[[sciencejournals](https://sciencejournals.ru/view-article/?j=prikbio&y=2023&v=59&n=2&a=PrikBio2301008Ryabtseva)]

|Mechanism of action|Scientific rationale|
|---|---|
|Suppression of pathogen toxins (C. difficile, E. coli)|Synthesis of protease that inactivates toxins [[cyberleninka](https://cyberleninka.ru/article/n/chto-novogo-v-diagnostike-i-lechenii-antibiotikoassotsiirovannyh-diarey-u-detey)]|
|Strengthening the intestinal barrier|Stimulation of epithelial cell proliferation and maturation [[hsvikopethealth](https://hsvikopethealth.com/ru/news/what-probiotics-are-good-for-cats/)]|
|Reduction of diarrhea duration|Reduction by ~24 hours in adults and children, similarly in animals [[pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11176121/)]|
|Prevention of antibiotic-associated diarrhea|Risk reduction of 53% with prophylaxis [[applied-research](https://applied-research.ru/article/view?id=11927)]|

For animals, **S. boulardii for cats during diet change** and **S. boulardii for dogs during diet change** is used at dosages established by veterinary protocols (typically 250–500 mg/animal 1–2 times daily during diet transition).[[hsvikopethealth](https://hsvikopethealth.com/ru/news/what-probiotics-are-good-for-cats/)]

**Probiotic during diet change** is especially effective in combination with prebiotic fiber (e.g., Fibersol-2), which further improves microbial balance and reduces the negative consequences of abrupt diet change in cats.[[zooinform](https://zooinform.ru/vete/issledovanie-pokazalo-chto-dobavki-s-probiotikami-i-kletchatkoj-uluchshayut-pishhevarenie-u-koshek/)]

## How Long Does Adaptation Take

It has been experimentally established: with a gradual transition of dogs to a new diet over one week, the microbiome fully stabilizes within **6–10 days**. In cats, the adaptation period is similar — 7–10 days with a gradual transition.[[pcr](https://pcr.news/novosti/mikrobiom-sobaki-menyaetsya-v-techenie-nedeli-pri-smene-korma/)]

Recommended protocol:

1. **Days 1–2**: 25% new food + 75% old food
    
2. **Days 3–4**: 50% new + 50% old
    
3. **Days 5–6**: 75% new + 25% old
    
4. **Day 7+**: 100% new food
    

During the entire adaptation period, a **probiotic during diet change** with **S. boulardii** is given, reducing the risk of **diet change diarrhea** by 40–60%.[[applied-research](https://applied-research.ru/article/view?id=11927)]
