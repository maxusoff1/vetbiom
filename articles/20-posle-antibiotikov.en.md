# S. boulardii for Recovery After Antibiotics: A Scientific Review with Evidence Base

Antibiotic therapy inevitably disrupts the balance of intestinal microbiota, which can lead to antibiotic-associated diarrhea (AAD) and dysbiosis. The probiotic **S. boulardii** (Saccharomyces boulardii CNCM I-745) has demonstrated high efficacy in **recovery after antibiotics**, reducing the risk of AAD from 18.7% to 8.5% (RR 0.47, 95% CI 0.38–0.57) in a systematic review of 21 randomized clinical trials.[[rmj](https://www.rmj.ru/articles/gastroenterologiya/Probiotiki_pri_gastroenterologicheskih_zabolevaniyah_v_centre_vnimaniya_Saccharomyces_boulardii_CNCM_I-745/)]

## When to Start Recovery

**S. boulardii recovery** can begin on the same day antibiotics are prescribed — the yeast probiotic is naturally resistant to all antibiotics due to its non-bacterial nature and does not require a time interval between doses. Concurrent administration of antibiotics with **S. boulardii** statistically significantly reduces AAD development by more than 50%.[[health-ua](https://health-ua.com/article/43848-primenenie-Saccharomyces-boulardii-vsovremennoj-klinicheskoj-praktike)]

In a double-blind randomized study with 388 patients, the diarrhea rate in the group taking the **probiotic after antibiotics** during antibiotic therapy was only 4.5% compared to 17.5% in the placebo group. The World Gastroenterology Organisation (WGO) recommends using the _Saccharomyces boulardii_ CNCM I-745 strain for the treatment of multiple diarrhea-related disorders.[[lvrach](https://www.lvrach.ru/2009/05/9228940)]

Key advantage: **S. boulardii** reaches a stable population level within 3 days of administration and is no longer detectable in stool within 2–5 days after discontinuation, confirming its safety and lack of colonization.[[rmj](https://www.rmj.ru/articles/gastroenterologiya/Probiotiki_pri_gastroenterologicheskih_zabolevaniyah_v_centre_vnimaniya_Saccharomyces_boulardii_CNCM_I-745/)]

## How Long the Course Lasts

The **recovery after antibiotics** course with **S. boulardii** typically lasts 5–10 days during antibiotic therapy plus 3–7 days after its completion. In the systematic review, the number needed to treat (NNT) to prevent one case of AAD is 10 (95% CI 9–13).[[rmj](https://www.rmj.ru/articles/gastroenterologiya/Probiotiki_pri_gastroenterologicheskih_zabolevaniyah_v_centre_vnimaniya_Saccharomyces_boulardii_CNCM_I-745/)]

Research shows that **S. boulardii** reduces the rate of antibiotic-associated diarrhea by 2–4 times in various infections requiring antibiotic therapy. For treating _Clostridium difficile_-associated diarrhea, _Saccharomyces boulardii_ was proven effective as monotherapy.[[lvrach](https://www.lvrach.ru/2009/05/9228940)]

Practical application in veterinary medicine confirms: **S. boulardii** after antibiotic therapy significantly accelerated the restoration of normal intestinal microbiota during dysbiosis in animals. For **animal microbiota**, the probiotic provides protection against dysbiosis during antibiotic use without altering normal gut microbiota composition.[[nogr](https://www.nogr.org/jour/article/viewFile/442/441)]

## Microbiota Support

The mechanism of action of the **probiotic after antibiotics** involves several pathways:

- **Protection against microbiota loss**: preventing the loss of beneficial microorganisms during diarrhea and excessive growth of pathogenic bacteria leads to faster recovery of normal gut microbiota[[saccharomycesboulardii](https://www.saccharomycesboulardii.com/chto-takoe-saharomicety-bulardi-cncm-i-745/mekhanizm-dejstviya/)]
    
- **Competitive displacement**: direct competitive displacement of pathogenic microorganisms from the gut[[health-ua](https://health-ua.com/article/43848-primenenie-Saccharomyces-boulardii-vsovremennoj-klinicheskoj-praktike)]
    
- **Immunomodulation**: regulation of immune response — acting as an immunostimulant or reducing pro-inflammatory reactions[[cyberleninka](https://cyberleninka.ru/article/n/klinicheskaya-effektivnost-preparatov-na-osnove-probioticheskih-shtammov-saccharomyces-boulardii)]
    
- **Antitoxic effect**: targeting pathogenic bacterial strains, preventing their attachment to intestinal cells and penetration[[saccharomycesboulardii](https://www.saccharomycesboulardii.com/chto-takoe-saharomicety-bulardi-cncm-i-745/mekhanizm-dejstviya/)]
    

Thanks to its diverse mechanisms of action, **S. boulardii** promotes faster restoration of normal intestinal microbiota during dysbiosis. Studies confirm that **S. boulardii** does not alter normal intestinal microbiota composition, except for a specific reduction in pathogen bacterial load.[[nogr](https://www.nogr.org/jour/article/viewFile/442/441)]

For **animal microbiota**, the probiotic is especially valuable: it provides protection against dysbiosis during antibiotic therapy and accelerates recovery after an antibiotic course. Over 100 clinical studies have confirmed the efficacy of _Saccharomyces boulardii_ CNCM I-745 for treating diarrhea, preventing its development, and **recovery after antibiotics**.[[saccharomycesboulardii](https://www.saccharomycesboulardii.com/chto-takoe-saharomicety-bulardi-cncm-i-745/nauchnye-dannye/)]
